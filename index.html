How To Survive A Photoshoot

Brand image

The goal of the photoshoot was to capture a concept, to tell the story of a mini collection created by a team of designers.

Analog Legacy, a collection that want to enhance details of construction and durability of each piece. Traditional skills and research for innovation are combine to show cultural and emotional values linked to a garment acquisition and transmission.

The concept

What is the story we want to tell and how to infuse it in a minimal setting in a studio. What garments should we showcase and in what order. Decide backdrops, lighting, the key looks, to create the feeling we are pursuing.

Pre-prod meeting

Explore

Do photos of the garments on someone to explore poses that would show the vision of the collection. Document all ideas, looks make-up, light etc to be able to have a cohesive ensemble.

Moodboards

Create mood boards for every elements that needs to be validated ahead of time:

  • model
  • hair
  • makeup
  • shoes
  • accessories
  • poses
  • furnitures and other props
  • background
  • light
  • overall ambiance

Think ahead

Models

Looking for models that would fit the desired effect and vision but also that would be affordable in a very tight budget. Look for model agencies and ask for upcoming or new models that could do a photoshoot at a budget cost or in exchange of the photos made by a professional for their portfolio. Also look at friends, family, social media and all means of networking.

On D Day

Schedule

Everything should be planned and scheduled so that every membre of the team in charge of the photoshoot knows what to do and when.

In the case of the analog photoshoot, on the day of the photoshoot we organized each step of the day:

  • Picking up furniture
  • Getting authorizations to bring furniture to the studio
  • Welcoming the models and making sure they had everything they needed
  • Meeting with the photographer and styling assistant before the shoot starts
  • Set-up the studio with props and organise a changing area, a food and rest area
  • Get the clothes of the collection ready on a rack in order for each scene.
